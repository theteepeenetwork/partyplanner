# Product Overview

## Vision

PartyPlanner aims to simplify event planning by allowing customers to discover, compare, quote, and book multiple event services through a single platform.

The platform is designed to reduce friction for both customers and suppliers through structured pricing and automated quote generation.

---

## Core USP

### Automated Event Quotes

Traditional event marketplaces focus on listings and enquiries.

PartyPlanner focuses on automated quoting.

Suppliers define structured pricing rules once.

Customers receive instant pricing based on:

* Guest numbers
* Duration
* Packages
* Optional extras
* Quantities
* Coverage rules

This reduces manual administration while improving pricing transparency.

## Pricing Philosophy

Pricing should be:

* Structured
* Consistent
* Machine-readable
* Transparent

The platform intentionally avoids vague pricing structures wherever possible.

### Guest-Based Pricing

Uses fixed numerical ranges.

Avoid:

* Up to 100 guests
* 100+ guests

Prefer:

* 1–50
* 51–100
* 101–150

This enables reliable automation.

### Duration-Based Pricing

Supports:

* Hourly rates
* Daily rates
* Time blocks

### Packages

Suppliers may optionally create:

* Standard
* Premium
* Deluxe

Packages may vary by:

* Price
* Duration
* Included services

---

## Coverage Rules

Suppliers define:

* Base location
* Coverage radius
* Optional travel charges

Suppliers outside the required coverage area are excluded from standard search results.

Customers may still access suppliers directly if a link is shared.

Coverage information is not publicly displayed.

---

## Marketplace Principles

### Fair Exposure

The marketplace should avoid becoming pay-to-win.

Premium suppliers may receive:

* Enhanced visibility
* Featured placements
* Marketing opportunities

However:

* Exposure should rotate fairly
* Visibility should not be monopolised
* Search quality should remain relevant

### Transparency

Customers should receive:

* Clear pricing
* Clear booking information
* Clear coverage expectations

### Efficiency

Suppliers should spend less time:

* Preparing quotes
* Responding to unsuitable enquiries
* Filtering non-viable requests

---

## User Journeys

### Customer

1. Create event
2. Define requirements
3. Discover suppliers
4. Receive pricing
5. Build event package
6. Submit booking requests
7. Complete payment

### Supplier

1. Register account
2. Create service
3. Configure pricing
4. Configure coverage
5. Receive opportunities
6. Accept or decline bookings

---

## Design Principles

The product should feel:

* Premium
* Organised
* Trustworthy
* Accessible
* Warm
* Professional

The platform is a marketplace, not a luxury event planning agency.

Users book services rather than consultations.
